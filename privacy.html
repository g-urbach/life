Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 2026  ·  Version 1.0

LifeTold is a memoir companion that helps you record, organise, and preserve your life story. We take your privacy seriously — your memories are personal, and how we handle them matters. This policy explains what data we collect, why, who we share it with, and what rights you have under the Australian Privacy Act 1988.

1. Who we are

LifeTold is operated as an independent product based in Australia. If you have questions about this policy, you can reach us via the Help tab → Get in touch inside the app, or at lifetold.app.

2. What information we collect

We do not collect: date of birth, gender, payment card details (printing is handled by Peecho directly), or device identifiers beyond what is needed for error logging.

3. How we use your information

  • Providing the service — storing and displaying your memoir entries, powering Gethro's conversations, generating AI-written story drafts.
  • Improving reliability — error logs and usage events help us identify and fix bugs.
  • Weekly email prompts — only if you opt in via Settings. You can turn this off at any time.
  • Responding to your contact messages — if you report a bug or privacy concern through the app, we'll follow up where needed.

We do not use your memoir content to train AI models, and we do not sell your data to any third party.

6. Your rights

Under the Australian Privacy Act 1988, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion — delete your account and all associated data directly in the app (Settings → Delete My Account & All Data), or contact us to request deletion.
  • Opt out — turn off weekly email prompts at any time in Settings.

To exercise any of these rights, use the Help tab → Get in touch → Privacy question or data request inside the app.

7. Cookies and local storage

LifeTold does not use advertising cookies. We use browser localStorage for:

  • Keeping you signed in (Supabase session token)
  • Remembering your dark/light mode preference
  • Autosaving your memoir draft while you type (cleared when you save or sign out)
  • Queuing edits made while offline (cleared when you sign out)

No memoir content persists in local storage after you sign out.

8. How to make a complaint

If you believe we have mishandled your personal information, please contact us first using the Help tab → Get in touch → Privacy question or data request. We aim to respond within 5 business days.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC):

  • Website: oaic.gov.au
  • Phone: 1300 363 992

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. If changes are material, you will be asked to re-accept the terms inside the app before continuing. The effective date at the top of this page will always reflect when it was last updated.
